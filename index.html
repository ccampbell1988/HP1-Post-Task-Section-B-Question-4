Physical Preparation Outline for the Season

1

Guiding Principle

"You are what you repeatedly do, excellence then, is not an act, but a habit." - Aristotle.

2

Seasonal Structure (Mesocycle Focus)

  • Broken into mesocycles (e.g., monthly blocks) considering game schedule, travel, recovery.
  • Training loads undulate based on game density.
3

Weekly Structure (Microcycle Focus)

  • Early Week: Recovery, low-intensity active recovery, mobility, moderate strength (if no mid-week game).
  • Mid-Week: Main strength/power session (if no games) or lighter activation (if mid-week game).
  • Late Week: Speed, power, game-specific activation, minimal off-ice work.
  • Game Days: Pre-game warm-up and activation only.
  • Post-Game: Immediate recovery protocols.
4

Long-Term Player Development (LTPD) Considerations

In-season focus is performance, but development continues via skill refinement and tactical understanding. Ongoing player assessment is key.

2. Warm-ups, Cool-downs & Recovery

Pre-Practice/Game Warm-up (20-30 mins)

  • Phase 1 (Off-Ice, 10-15 mins): General Mobility & Activation (Light aerobic, dynamic stretching, activation exercises).
  • Phase 2 (Off-Ice/On-Ice, 5-10 mins): Dynamic Movement & CNS Prep (Lunges, skips, sprints, medicine ball).
  • Phase 3 (On-Ice, 10-15 mins): Sport-Specific (Skating, puck control, passing, shooting, game-like simulations).

Cool-downs & Ongoing Recovery

Post-Practice/Game Cool-down (10-15 mins)

Purpose: Gradually decrease heart rate, reduce soreness, initiate recovery.

Components: Light aerobic activity, static stretching, foam rolling, rehydration, initial nutrition.

Recovery Procedures (Ongoing)

  • Sleep: 8+ hours quality sleep.
  • Nutrition & Hydration (see next section).
  • Active Recovery: Low-intensity on off-days.
  • Soft Tissue Work: Massage, physiotherapy.
  • Mental Recovery: Mindfulness, relaxation.
  • Injury Management: Prompt attention.

3. Performance Pillars

Fueling, Hydration, and Integrated Recovery

Hydration

  • Daily Focus: Consistent hydration; pale yellow urine.
  • Pre-Activity: Optimal status.
  • During Activity: Regular sips.
  • Post-Activity: Replenish fluids.

Nutrition

General Principles: Balanced whole foods. Carbs (fuel), Protein (repair), Healthy Fats.

  • Pre-Game/Practice Meal (2-4 hrs before): Digestible carbs, mod protein, low fat/fiber.
  • During Game: Digestible carbs (drinks, gels).
  • Post-Game/Practice Recovery (30-60 mins): Carbs & protein.

Integrated Recovery

  • Cool-down initiates recovery.
  • Prioritize sleep. Strategic napping can help.
  • Listen to the body; rest days are crucial.
  • Mental relaxation and stress management are vital.

Improving Critical Fitness Variables (CFVs)

CFVs: Skating speed/agility, puck control, shooting, strength, power, endurance.

Measurement & Evaluation In-Season

  • Needs Assessment: Pre-season testing & ongoing observation.
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Coach observation, player feedback (RPE, wellness), video, KPIs.
  • Minimal, Targeted Testing: Infrequent, strategic (e.g., jumps, sprints).

Improving CFVs In-Season

  • Strength Training (1-2/wk): Maintain. Hockey-specific. High intensity, reduced volume.
  • Power Training: Olympic lifts, plyos, med ball.
  • Speed & Agility: Short, intense. Acceleration, deceleration, COD. Often on-ice.
  • Conditioning: Via high-tempo practices/games. Supplementary: short, intense intervals if needed.
  • Skill-Based Fitness: Drills combining skill with physical demands.
  • Deliberate Practice: Focus on weaknesses with high effort; practice under simulated stress.

4. Program Support & Tracking

Performance Metrics Overview

Illustrative radar chart. Actual tracking would be individualized.

Injury Prevention

  • Comprehensive warm-ups & cool-downs.
  • Strength training for stability/balance.
  • Mobility and flexibility work.
  • Load management (key communication).
  • Individualized approach.
  • Proper technique.
  • Emphasize recovery strategies.
  • Address muscular imbalances.
  • Proper equipment.
  • Player monitoring (wellness, RPE).

Managing Support Staff

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Head Coach: Overall direction.
  • S&C Coach: Off-ice programs.
  • Medical Staff: Injury management.
  • Others: Mental Perf. Coach, Nutritionist, Assistants.

Communication Strategy

  • Regular all-staff meetings.
  • Open daily communication.
  • Shared info platforms.
  • Consistent messaging.
  • Feedback loops.

Utilizing Outside Resources

  • Clear communication channels.
  • Provider understands team program.

Relevant Templates

  • Monthly Training/Season Plan Calendar: Maps schedule & workload.
  • Player Assessment/Evaluation Sheet: Tracks individual skills & CFV progress.
  • Individual Drill/Practice Plan Segment: Designs targeted practice segments.
  • Personal Development Plan (PDP) / Habit Tracker: For individual goals.
  • Game Card: Bench management, quick notes.
  • Off-Ice Training Session Plan: Detailed workout structure.
